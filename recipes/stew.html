Nigerian Tomato Stew

Description

Nigerian Tomato Stew is a rich, spicy, and flavorful sauce made with blended tomatoes, red bell peppers, and onions. It is simmered in oil and often cooked with beef or chicken for added depth.

This classic dish is a staple in many Nigerian homes and pairs perfectly with white rice, boiled yams, or fried plantains. It’s loved for its bold flavors and versatility.

Ingredients

  • 6 large tomatoes
  • 3 red bell peppers (tatashe)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (ata rodo)
  • 1 large onion (half for blending, half for frying)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable or palm oil
  • 1-2 seasoning cubes (Maggi/Knorr)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • Meat or chicken (pre-cooked with stock)

Steps

  1. Blend the tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnets, and half the onion until smooth.
  2. Pour the blended mix into a pot and boil until most of the water evaporates (about 15–20 mins).
  3. In another pot, heat the oil and sauté the sliced onions.
  4. Add the boiled tomato mixture and fry for 10–15 minutes, stirring often to avoid burning.
  5. Season with thyme, curry, salt, and seasoning cubes. Add your cooked meat/chicken and some stock.
  6. Simmer everything together for another 10–15 minutes until the oil floats on top and the stew thickens.
  7. Serve hot with rice, yam, plantain, or any dish of your choice.
